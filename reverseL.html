Reverse Logistics

What is Reverse Logistics?

Reverse logistics involves the management of product returns, recycling, and disposal. In the context of e-waste, reverse logistics refers to the process of collecting end-of-life electronic products and transporting them to recycling facilities for proper disposal and recycling.



Our Reverse Logistics Process

Collection

We partner with manufacturers, retailers, and consumers to collect electronic waste through various channels, including drop-off locations, mail-back programs, and collection events.

Transportation

Our logistics team manages the transportation of e-waste from collection points to our recycling facilities. We utilize efficient routing and transportation methods to minimize environmental impact.

Tracking and Reporting

We employ advanced tracking systems to monitor the movement of e-waste throughout the reverse logistics chain. This allows us to provide accurate reporting on the quantities and types of e-waste collected and recycled.

Disposal and Recycling

At our state-of-the-art recycling facilities, we employ environmentally friendly methods to safely dispose of and recycle e-waste. We recover valuable materials such as metals, plastics, and glass, while minimizing waste and pollution.
